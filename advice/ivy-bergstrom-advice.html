Hi, my name is Ivy and I have some advice for you and I want you to fre@king believe in yourself!

Know your resoures!

Luckily, you are not the first person to learn how to code. There are hundreds of thousands of people who have asked the same questions that you will come across. Know where you can go online toget this questions answered. The resources are *endless*.

If you are a fellow skillcrusher, take advantage of the skillcrush slack channel. If not, check out stack overflow, and just google blogs and tutorials from other coders.

Make note of weak areas and develop plans to improve them

For example, I really struggled with Github. To help improve, I made flashcards of differet commands, vocabulary, and everything I had a hard time retaining.

Another way to help wrap your mind around something challening is to write a small quiz for yourself, consisting of questions you have, then reasearch the answers.

I hope this helps! Don't be discouraged by the challenge, you're doing something new and amazing.

Take opportunities to challenge yourself

And oh my god, have fun with it! Don't settle on your websites, keep trying until you love the way it looks.

Troubleshoot troubleshoot troubleshoot.

Like, I really cannot stress that enough. Whenever you troubleshoot something, and you will, write down what was wrong and how you fixed it.

It helps build your semantic knowledge, which helps you have a more complete image of what you're doing grand scheme, rather than just in the immediate project you are currently working on. It is good for your brain.
